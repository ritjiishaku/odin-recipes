Classic Spaghetti Carbonara

Description

Classic Spaghetti Carbonara is a rich and creamy Italian pasta dish made with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and pepper. It’s simple, yet indulgent, and can be prepared in just 20 minutes. The perfect balance of salty pancetta, creamy egg mixture, and Parmesan creates an unforgettable meal!

Ingredients

  • 12 oz spaghetti
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 4 oz pancetta, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional, for garnish)

Steps

  1. Cook the spaghetti in a large pot of salted boiling water according to package instructions, usually 8–10 minutes, until al dente.
  2. While the pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the diced pancetta and cook until crispy, about 4-5 minutes. Remove from the heat and set aside.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, Parmesan, and a generous amount of black pepper. Set aside.
  4. Drain the pasta, reserving 1/2 cup of pasta water. Add the hot pasta directly to the pan with pancetta, mixing well to coat the pasta in the rendered fat.
  5. Slowly pour the egg mixture over the hot pasta, tossing quickly to create a creamy sauce. Add reserved pasta water if the sauce seems too thick.
  6. Serve immediately, garnished with additional Parmesan, fresh parsley, and a bit more black pepper. Enjoy!
